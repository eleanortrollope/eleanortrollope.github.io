I am currently a DPhil student in Machine Learning at the University of Oxford as part of the CDT in Autonomous Intelligent Machines & Systems (AIMS). I am supervised by Prof. Yee-Whye Teh (Oxford Computational Statistics and Machine Learning), Prof. Stephen Roberts (Machine Learning Research Group), and Dr. Tim Rudner (University of Toronto). My research focuses on developing novel AI methods for scientific discovery, with an emphasis on building robust and generalisable learning algorithms. I am particularly interested in generative models for molecular design and drug discovery, with a focus on uncertainty, inductive biases, and reliable generalisation.
